  • Process

I design with salt & pepper approach. This means my designs consist of concepts from the opposites ends of the spectrum, which I create into one artistic work. Finding the balance in a piece makes the design stable. I feel in art, as well as life, you cannot have one without the other. My work reflects these idealized statements in “Sweet but Dark,” “Bound yet Free,” and “Technological but Textural.” Art cannot happen without the designer;

thus I enjoy adding a bit of me in each piece while still identifying with the objectives of a project. I aim for substance and breadth of meaning in a piece to give the audience a conversation with the object itself. My art is meant to give substance for dialogue, beyond the “I like it” response. As an audience is meant to interact and experience my art, I create the language of experience with my designs.

Process Planning

El Centro is a nonprofit organization in the Chippewa Valley. Their mission of connecting and unifying cultural differences in the American and Latino community in their area needed a brand that supported them. To begin this process, we explored what was culturally acceptable and what was offensive. After, exploring what to avoid in the brand we focused on culturally connecting imagery. Here is just a sampling of the 50-logo sketches we produced for the brand.

Designed Process

Next, I began focusing on two central ideas. I wanted a neutral cultural identity that could not be limited to a starting identity feel so that there would be room for growth in the organization. I also focused on patterns from their cultural roots. I wanted to create a tapestry to tell their story and the value of family stated in their mission. I focused on the brand to feel contemporary, professional, and family-oriented.

El Centro Brand

Once deciding on the final logo, I created secondary imagery to help tell the story. This included the final pattern that gave the needed texture. The result was an expression of curves in a celebratory manner. Here are just a few of the final pieces we did for them.

Everything was produced in a professional manner, since this was an actual client that intended to use it for their identity. Before our work samples were presented to the client, our professor had a series of eliminations to narrow the twenty-four students down to the top eight. I was among these. The client's liaison and other peers described my work as "clean-lined, flowing, with a lot to offer." My objective of making it versatile was also recognized as successful, though the client opted for brand that displayed more of a "festive style" in their central logo.
